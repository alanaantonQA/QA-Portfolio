### General Impressions

Overall, the game feels really good and genuinely fun to play.  
The core loop is engaging, the systems are easy to understand, and I found myself wanting to keep doing more runs even after encountering some issues. The foundation is very solid and shows a lot of potential.

---

### Audio & Music

- There is a static noise in the background when the game starts. It disappears after a while, but it is noticeable at launch.  
- The music is really good and fits the game atmosphere very well.  
- However, in several matches the music stopped completely and never came back.

---

### Gameplay & Mechanics

- The spellbook is very well designed, and having it accessible from the menu is a great decision.  
- Spell and aspect explanations are clear and easy to understand.  
- At the beginning, it is not entirely clear whether the player can or should go down the tower, which may cause hesitation.  
- Spells (especially Fireball) often collide with nearby objects such as slopes, rocks, plants, and props, preventing them from being cast correctly at close range.  
- I feel there should be a basic low-damage attack available at any time for free.  
- I couldn‚Äôt change the position of abilities; being able to rearrange them would be helpful.

---

### Difficulty & Progression

- The difficulty feels high from the start, especially for new players.  
- This is noticeable in the first level, but from the third level onward it becomes much harder and many players might struggle to progress.  
- A small tutorial would help, and it might be good if the first ‚Äúdeals‚Äù available were less punishing so players can get used to the systems gradually.

### Final Thoughts

Despite the issues mentioned, I really enjoyed the experience.
